NF-kappa B signaling pathway

Pathway Name: NF-kappa B signaling pathway - Homo sapiens (human)
Pathway Source: KEGG Pathway
Genes involved in Wnt signaling pathway: ----

Nuclear factor-kappa B (NF-kappa B) is the generic name of a family of transcription factors that function as dimers and regulate genes involved in immunity, inflammation and cell survival. There are several pathways leading to NF-kappa B-activation. Canonical and non-canonical pathway leading to the activation of NF-kB. The canonical pathway is induced by cytokines such as Tumor Necrosis Factor (TNFalpha), Interleukin-1 (IL-1). The activation results in the phosphorylation of IkBalpha by the IKK complex, leading to its ubiquitylation (Ub) and subsequent degradation by the 26S proteasome. The RelA/p50 complex is free to translocate to the nucleus to activate the transcription of target genes. The non-canonical pathway results in the activation of IKKalpha by the NF-kB-inducing kinase (NIK) after stimulation by TNF cytokine family. The formation of the complex NIK-IKKalpha-p100 leads to the phosphorylation of the p100 subunit. This results in 26S proteasome dependent processing of p100 to p52, which can lead to the activation of p52-RelB that target distinct kB element and induce the transcription of target genes.
