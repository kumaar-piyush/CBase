Colon Cancer, also known as colorectal cancer (CRC), is a type of cancer that arises from the growth of cells in a part of the large intestine called the colon. It usually develops from polyps or growths in the inner lining of the colon. It is the third most common cancer and has the second highest mortality rate reported worldwide. More than 90% of CRCs are adenocarcinoma, a malignant neoplasm that develops from glandular epithelial cells of the colon and rectum.

According to The ColonoBase, using integrated bioinformatics and statistical approaches, we identified common differentially expressed genes (cDEGs) involved in the initiation and progression of colorectal cancer (CRC) from multiple transcriptomics datasets. This database catalogs a variety of data types related to colorectal cancer research, including genomic data, clinical data, and various biomolecular datasets, facilitating a comprehensive analysis and understanding of the disease.

Pathway data was sourced from the Reactome and KEGG databases. The protein-protein interaction network of common differentially expressed genes (cDEGs) was constructed using the STRING database and BioGRID. However, the results obtained are dispersed across thousands of scientific articles and websites. A resource that integrates all these heterogeneous data would greatly aid the biomedical research community in the fight against colorectal cancer.

According to updated estimates from the International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC), global cancer statistics for the year 2022 reveal that lung cancer was the most frequently diagnosed cancer, followed by breast cancer and colorectal cancer in 2022, , responsible for almost 2.5 million new cases, or one in eight cancers worldwide (12.4% of all cancers globally), followed by cancers of the female breast (11.6%), colorectum (9.6%), prostate (7.3%), and stomach (4.9%). The estimated incidence rates of colorectal cancer are highest in Australia/New Zealand, Western Africa, and South-Central Asia.
